Individuals often first notice this condition when observing straight objects, such as mini-blinds, which appear wavy or bent. Objects may also appear closer or further away than they actually are.

Metamorphopsia is commonly associated with macular degeneration, particularly age-related macular degeneration with choroidal neovascularization. Other conditions that can present with metamorphopsia include pathological myopia, presumed ocular histoplasmosis syndrome, choroidal rupture, and multifocal choroiditis. It is a symptom of several eye disorders involving the retina or macula, including:

  • Age-related macular degeneration
  • Epiretinal membrane and vitreomacular traction
  • Posterior vitreous detachment
  • Macular hole
